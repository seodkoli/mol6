Frequently Asked Questions

How do you negotiate with a hard money lender?

Negotiating with a hard money lender involves discussing loan terms such as interest rates, loan amounts, repayment schedules, and fees. Borrowers can leverage factors such as the property's value, their creditworthiness, and the current market conditions to negotiate favorable terms.

What is another name for hard money lender?

Another name for a hard money lender is a private money lender, as they both provide financing based on the collateral of real estate rather than the borrower's creditworthiness.

What is the difference between a private money lender and a hard money lender?

Private money lenders and hard money lenders are often used interchangeably, but some distinguish them by the types of loans they offer. Hard money lenders usually provide short-term loans secured by real estate, while private money lenders may offer a broader range of financing options beyond real estate.

What is 3 points on hard money loan?

In hard money lending, "3 points" typically refers to a fee equal to 3% of the loan amount charged by the lender. This fee is usually paid upfront and is separate from the interest charged on the loan.
