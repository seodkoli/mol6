Frequently Asked Questions

What is the hard money scenario?

The hard money scenario refers to the situation where borrowers opt for hard money loans due to their inability to secure financing from traditional lenders, often because of factors such as poor credit history, urgent funding needs, or unconventional property types.

How is a hard money loan calculated?

A hard money loan is calculated based on the value of the property being used as collateral, known as the loan-to-value (LTV) ratio. Lenders typically offer a percentage of the property's appraised value, with higher LTV ratios resulting in larger loan amounts.

Why is it called a hard money loan?

Hard money loans are called so because they are secured by "hard" assets such as real estate, which serve as collateral for the loan. Unlike traditional loans that rely heavily on the borrower's creditworthiness, hard money loans prioritize the value of the collateral.

How do you write a hard money offer?

To write a hard money offer, borrowers typically provide details about the property they intend to use as collateral, their requested loan amount, desired terms (such as interest rate and repayment schedule), and any additional information relevant to the loan transaction. It's essential to be clear, concise, and accurate in the offer.
