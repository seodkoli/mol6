Frequently Asked Questions

Can I lend money like a bank?

Yes, individuals can lend money like a bank by becoming private lenders or investors. However, lending money involves risks, and individuals should carefully evaluate potential borrowers, terms, and collateral to mitigate those risks.

What is a private lender?

A private lender is an individual or entity that provides loans to borrowers, often outside of traditional banking institutions. Private lenders may offer various types of financing, including hard money loans, personal loans, and business loans.

How is a hard money loan calculated?

A hard money loan is calculated based on the value of the property being used as collateral, known as the loan-to-value (LTV) ratio. Lenders typically offer a percentage of the property's appraised value, with higher LTV ratios resulting in larger loan amounts.

What does hard mean money?

"Hard money" refers to currency or assets that have intrinsic value and are widely accepted as a medium of exchange. It contrasts with "soft money," which includes forms of currency like fiat money that rely on government regulation and trust.
