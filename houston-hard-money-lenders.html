Frequently Asked Questions

Is hard money same as cash?

While hard money and cash are both forms of currency or assets, they differ in terms of liquidity and legal tender status. Hard money typically refers to assets like gold or real estate, while cash typically refers to fiat currency issued by governments and widely accepted as a medium of exchange.

Is Bitcoin hard money?

Bitcoin is often considered "hard money" due to its limited supply and decentralized nature, similar to gold. However, it differs from traditional hard money like real estate because it lacks physical presence and is entirely digital.

What does hard money lender mean?

A hard money lender is a financial institution or individual that provides short-term loans secured by real estate. These loans are typically used by borrowers who cannot obtain financing through traditional channels due to factors such as poor credit history or the need for quick funding.
