Frequently Asked Questions

What does hard mean money?

"Hard money" refers to currency or assets that have intrinsic value and are widely accepted as a medium of exchange. It contrasts with "soft money," which includes forms of currency like fiat money that rely on government regulation and trust.

What are typical hard money terms?

Typical hard money loan terms include higher interest rates than traditional loans, shorter repayment periods (often ranging from a few months to a few years), and lower loan-to-value ratios. Fees such as origination points and closing costs may also be involved.

What credit score is needed for a hard money loan?

Credit score requirements for hard money loans vary, but they typically focus more on the value of the property being used as collateral rather than the borrower's credit score. However, a higher credit score may still improve the borrower's chances of approval and influence the loan terms.
